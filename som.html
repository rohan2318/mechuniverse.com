Experiment No.01

Tensile test

(Mild steel specimen)

Aim :

To study the mechanical properties of Mild Steel specimen under tension load.

Description :

In tension test ends of a test piece are fixed into grips connected to a straining device and to a load measuring device. The test involves straining a test piece by tensile force generally to fracture for the purpose of determining one or more of the mechanical properties.

Tensile test

(Cast Iron specimen)

Aim :

To study the mechanical properties of Cast Iron specimen under tension load.

Description :

In this test ends of a test piece are fixed into grips connected to a straining device and to a load measuring device. The test involves straining a test piece by tensile force generally to fracture for the purpose of determining one or more of the mechanical properties. Brittle materials such as cast iron do not have a yield point, and do not strain-harden. Therefore, the ultimate strength and breaking strength are the same.

Aim :

To study the mechanical properties of Mild Steel specimen under compression

In compression test the material experiences opposing forces that push inward upon the specimen from opposite sides or is otherwise compressed, squashed, crushed, or flattened.

Compression test

(Cast Iron specimen)

Aim :

To study the mechanical properties of Cast Iron specimen under compression

Description :

A compression test is a method for determining the behaviour of materials under a compressive load. Compression tests are conducted by loading the test specimen between two plates and then applying a force to the specimen by moving the crossheads together. Here the test specimen is compressed and deformation versus the applied load is recorded.

Shear test

Aim :

To determine experimentally, the ultimate shear strength in double shear of mild steel rod.

DDescription :

In direct shear test, the shearing stress is considered as uniformly distributed over the entire cross section. The shear force is applied by a suitable test rig, two different cases of shearing may arise; i.e., single shear and double shear.

Experiment No.05

Torsion test

Aim :

To study the mechanical properties of mild steel under torsion.

Description :

In torsion testing the circular bar is placed in the machine such a way that its longitudinal axis coincides with the axis of the grips and so that it remains straight during the test.Troptometer is used to measure the twist to an accuracy of one minute.
